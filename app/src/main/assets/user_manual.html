RoktoDhara User & Technical Manual

Empowering Blood Donation Through Data & Community

1. Introduction

RoktoDhara is a state-of-the-art blood donation management system designed to connect donors with patients in real-time. By leveraging cloud synchronization and local geospatial processing, we ensure that help is always just a few taps away.

2. Database Ecosystem Analysis

The application operates on a hybrid database architecture to provide "Offline-First" reliability.

Cloud Layer (Firebase Firestore)

All real-time data is handled by Firestore, using three primary document trees:

  • Users: Stores authentication metadata, donation eligibility, and device tokens for mass alerts.
  • Blood Requests: Tracks patient details, blood group requirements, and fulfillment status.
  • Health Facilities: A master list of certified Hospitals and Blood Banks nationwide.

Local Layer (SQLite Persistence)

To support users without reliable internet, the app maintains a local cache (rokto_dhara_cache.db):

  • Sync Logic: Whenever you are online, the app automatically pulls updates and overwrites the local SQLite tables.
  • Tables: donors, hospitals, and blood_banks are stored locally to allow distance sorting and searching in Offline Mode.

3. Core Features Guide

3.1 Finding Donors

The "Available Donors" section uses live geospatial data. When you search, the app calculates the distance between your current GPS coordinates and the donor's registered location.

3.2 Creating Requests

When you create a New Request, the system broadcasts a notification to all users matching the requested blood group within your division. Premium requests are highlighted in the feed for higher visibility.

3.3 Offline Dashboard

Accessible from the login screen, the Offline Mode allows you to browse cached contact details for hospitals and blood banks even if you have no data connection.

4. Administrative Controls

For administrators, the portal provides high-level oversight:

  • Mass Alerts: Send emergency broadcasts to all registered users simultaneously.
  • Moderation: Banning or upgrading users to premium status based on their donation history.
  • Facility Management: Adding or editing hospital and blood bank metadata directly.

5. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Why do I need to agree to terms?
A: To maintain community safety and ensure valid data for medical emergencies.

Q: Is my location shared?
A: only for the purpose of distance calculation between you and those in need. Precise coordinates are never displayed to regular users.
